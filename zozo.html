From ZOZO, a second-year Aviation Business Management Student, student id: 67628169

Q: What do you consider must-know things as a student?
A: I consider that students should know activity points are very important for graduation, so you need to join events regularly. You should also know how to use Microsoft Teams and Line because most communication and assignments happen there. Another important thing is the student ID card, it's useful for entering buildings, borrowing things, and sometimes getting discounts.
Q: What do you consider must-do things as a student?
A: You should always submit assignments on time and check announcements regularly. If you're an international student, make sure to handle your visa and 90-day reporting on time. Moreover, you need to try to participate in activities early to collect enough points. Managing your time well is very important, especially when you have group projects.
Q: What important items should students bring from their home country that are difficult to find here?
A: I think bringing traditional clothes, food, and unique things is a good idea because there are cultural events in IC, CMRU. Then, bring any specific medicine or skincare products like Myanmar traditional Tha Na Khar you are used to, since some brands may not be available here.
Q: How do internships and research projects work in 3rd and 4th year?
A: From what I heard, in 3rd or 4th year, students need to do an internship related to aviation or business. Your performance is important, and if you fail or get a bad evaluation, you may need to redo it. There is also a research project or thesis, which is a big part of your final grade.
Q: What do students mean when they say studying at CMRU gets harder? Does it differ by major?
A: It gets harder because there are more assignments, group work, and presentations. Some teachers may ask questions on the spot, so you need to be prepared. It also depends on the major, but for ABM, communication and practical skills are more important.
Q: How did you find affordable accommodation?
A: I found accommodation through websites and online groups. Many students recommend places near the university because it's more convenient.
Q: If you live in a dorm, how did you secure a spot, and what is it like living in one?
A: I don't live in a dorm, but I heard it can be crowded and you may have to share a room with 3 or 4 roommates.
Q: Where do you usually shop for school clothing, regular clothing, groceries, and daily necessities?
A: I usually shop online via Lazada and Shopee for many things and local markets for groceries because they are cheaper and more affordable than shops at the shopping center. For clothes, I sometimes go to secondhand shops because they are reasonable. I wish I had known about more cheap local stores like Looker local brand earlier to save money because the designs are trendy and fashionable, and the prices are reasonable. Sometimes they offer discounts.
Q: What are or were your favorite subjects and why?
A: I like subjects related to aviation and management like Customer Service, Finance, and Accounting because they are interesting and useful for my future career.
Q: What are or were your favorite student activities or experiences, and why?
A: I enjoy joining university events because I can meet new people and gain experience. Some festivals and activity days are also really fun. Among them, I like the Water Festival the most because we can splash water on each other, get some free food, and have lots of fun.
Q: What do you wish you had known about your major and year beforehand?
A: I wish I had known that there would be a lot of group work and presentations. And sometimes communication can be challenging, so teamwork is very important.
Q: What is your dress code? How does it change over the years?
A: We usually wear a white shirt with navy blue pants for 1st year. For the 2nd year, we changed to a pilot shirt with navy pants. For 3rd and 4th years, we have to wear an ABM suit. Activity shirts are allowed only when IC has activities.
Q: What should students in your major and year prepare before starting?
A: Students should download Microsoft Teams, Line, and Google Translate. It's also good to have a laptop, sunscreen, and formal clothes for presentations or events. Learning the Thai language will be a plus.
Q: What does your typical workload or school day look like?
A: Easy days are when I only review lessons or do small assignments. Normal days include attending lectures and doing in-class work or homework and individual assignments. Difficult days involve group projects, presentations, and multiple assignments at the same time.
Q: How has your experience changed across semesters or years academically?
A: In my first year, things felt new and a bit confusing. In the second year, I feel more comfortable and better at managing my time and studies.
Q: Any additional advice, tips, or comments for future students?
A: Always check Line and announcements so you don't miss important information. Try to join activities early and manage your time well. Don't be afraid to ask questions or ask for help from friends. Check the exam timetable carefully before the actual exam starts. Do teacher evaluations, if you don't do it, you cannot see your grade. Make sure your MS Teams account must match with your student ID only.
